Your compliance obligations

Federal grant funding brings federal
language access obligations.

If your nonprofit receives any federal financial assistance — including federal grants, Head Start funding, CDBG grants, Title I subgrants, HHS program funds, or housing assistance — you are covered by Title VI and EO 13166 language access requirements. This includes translation of vital documents and provision of interpreter services for LEP individuals you serve.

📜

Title VI Compliance

Translate vital documents into the languages spoken by 5% or more of your service population. Provide qualified interpreters for service encounters. Develop and maintain a Language Access Plan. We do all of this.

🌐

Digital Accessibility

Nonprofit websites and digital content should meet WCAG 2.1 AA — both for legal compliance and because your community deserves accessible information. We audit and remediate nonprofit digital content at mission-friendly rates.

📋

Language Access Plans

A written Language Access Plan is the best evidence of good-faith compliance. Many federal funders now require one. We develop LAPs tailored to your service population, languages, and program types.
