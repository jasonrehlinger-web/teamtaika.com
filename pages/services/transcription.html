Transcription FAQ.

Verbatim transcription captures every word spoken including filler words ("um," "uh"), false starts, and repeated phrases. It is required for legal depositions and official proceedings. Edited transcription removes fillers and cleans up the text for readability while preserving the full meaning — preferred for interviews, research, and business communications.
Yes for multiple speakers — we include speaker identification labels (Speaker 1, Speaker 2, or named speakers if provided). For poor audio quality, we will flag it upfront and let you know if any sections are inaudible. We never guess at inaudible content; those sections are clearly marked as [inaudible].
All audio files are uploaded via TLS-encrypted portal, stored with AES-256 encryption, and handled under NDAs. Medical transcription uses HIPAA-compliant workflows. Legal transcription maintains attorney-client privilege protocols. We delete source files after delivery unless you instruct us to retain them.
