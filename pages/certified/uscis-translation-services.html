USCIS Translation FAQ

What does USCIS require for certified translations?

Under 8 CFR § 103.2(b)(3), any document submitted to USCIS that is not in English must be accompanied by a complete English translation. The translator must certify that the translation is accurate and complete, and that they are competent to translate from the source language into English. USCIS does not require that the translator be an ATA-certified member, but using an ATA-certified translator is the clearest way to demonstrate competence. The certification must include the translator's full name, signature, and date. Our Certificate of Accuracy meets all of these requirements.

What USCIS forms require translated documents?

Virtually every USCIS form that involves a foreign-born applicant requires certified translations of supporting documents. I-130 family petitions require birth and marriage certificates. I-485 adjustment of status requires birth certificates, marriage certificates, police clearances, and financial records. N-400 naturalization requires foreign birth records and any court or criminal records. K-1 fiancé visa petitions require the foreign national's personal civil documents. Asylum applications require personal statement evidence and country condition documentation. I-864 affidavit of support submissions may require financial document translations.

Will my application be rejected if the translation doesn't include a certification?

Yes — submitting an uncertified translation or a translation with an improper certification is grounds for a Request for Evidence (RFE) or denial. USCIS adjudicators are specifically trained to check translations for compliance with 8 CFR § 103.2(b)(3). An improperly certified translation does not count as a certified translation under the regulation. This is a common cause of RFEs for pro se applicants who use online translation services without understanding the certification requirement. All our translations include a compliant Certificate of Accuracy from day one.

Can you translate birth certificates for USCIS?

Yes — birth certificate translation is our most common USCIS service. We translate birth certificates from all countries and all languages, including countries with complex registration systems, handwritten certificates, or unusual formats. Our translators are experienced with document formats from every region of the world and know how to handle stamps, seals, annotations, and registration numbers that must be translated per USCIS rules. Every birth certificate translation includes the full Certificate of Accuracy and is formatted for USCIS submission.

Can I submit a digital certified translation to USCIS?

Yes. USCIS accepts digital (PDF) certified translations for forms submitted through the USCIS online filing system. For paper filings, you would print the PDF and include it in your packet. We deliver all translations as professionally formatted PDFs with the translation, original document copy, and Certificate of Accuracy in a single organized file. The PDF can be printed at any time and is accepted by USCIS whether filed online or by mail.
