Vietnamese in the United States

Vietnamese is among the top five LEP language communities served by U.S. government agencies.

Vietnamese is spoken by more than 1.5 million U.S. residents and is particularly concentrated in Texas, California, Washington, Virginia, and several southeastern states. Vietnamese-speaking communities have high rates of LEP and interact frequently with government benefits agencies, school districts, and healthcare providers. Vietnamese requires specialized diacritical mark handling and tonal language expertise — errors in Vietnamese translation are often not visible to English-speaking reviewers but are immediately apparent to native speakers.
