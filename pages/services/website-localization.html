Why translation alone isn't enough

A translated website that fails your audience
is worse than no translation at all.

Government agencies and healthcare systems face a specific localization problem: the web content that needs translation is also the content where cultural errors, accessibility failures, and layout breakdowns carry real consequences.

🌍

Translation is not localization

Machine translation produces literal text that sounds wrong to native speakers. Cultural adaptation, idioms, and register matter. A government portal that sounds like a robot alienates the community it's meant to serve.

♿

WCAG compliance must survive translation

Translated content that breaks alt text, heading structure, or reading order fails accessibility compliance. Our localization process preserves and validates WCAG 2.2 conformance in every target language.

↩️

RTL languages require layout changes

Arabic, Hebrew, and Urdu read right-to-left. A simple text swap isn't enough — layout and typography must adapt. We handle full RTL layout conversion as part of localization.

Frequently asked questions

Website localization questions, answered.

What's the difference between translation and localization?+
Translation converts words from one language to another. Localization goes further — it adapts meaning, tone, cultural references, idioms, date formats, units of measure, and visual layout so the content feels native to the target audience. A government portal that is merely translated may read as stilted or confusing; one that is localized reads as if it were written for that community originally.
Do you handle Arabic and Hebrew RTL layouts?+
Yes. Right-to-left languages including Arabic, Hebrew, Farsi, and Urdu require full layout mirroring — not just text substitution. We handle RTL CSS conversion, logical properties, and typography. If your CMS supports RTL natively we deliver content ready to activate; if it requires custom CSS we provide that as part of the project.
Will localization break my CMS?+
Not if the project is scoped correctly — which is why we audit your site and CMS before beginning. We deliver content in formats compatible with WordPress, Drupal, Sitecore, Salesforce CMS, and most custom platforms. We can work with exported content files, translation memory tools (XLIFF/TMX), or direct CMS integration depending on your setup.
Do you localize meta tags for multilingual SEO?+
Yes. Every page localization package includes title tags, meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, and image alt text localized for the target language. We also provide hreflang markup and a multilingual XML sitemap so search engines correctly index and serve language variants to users in each market.
Can you work with our existing CMS or do we need to export content?+
Both options are available. For most projects we recommend exporting content to XLIFF or a spreadsheet for translation, then reimporting — this approach is CMS-agnostic and reduces risk of accidental changes. For large enterprise sites with translation management plugins (WPML, Polylang, Drupal Translation, etc.) we can work directly within your existing workflow.
