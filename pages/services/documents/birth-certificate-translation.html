Why accuracy matters for birth certificates

One wrong character in a name — or a missing certification statement — can delay your entire petition.

USCIS processes millions of immigration petitions annually. Birth certificate translation errors are one of the top reasons for Requests for Evidence (RFEs).

📋

USCIS rejects translations missing the competency statement

USCIS 8 CFR 103.2(b)(3) requires every submitted translation to include a signed statement from the translator declaring competency in both languages. A translation that omits this — even a perfect translation — will be rejected.

🌍

Birth certificate formats vary by country

Mexico, Dominican Republic, China, India, and dozens of other countries use different civil registry formats. Our translators are country-specific specialists who know each format, field-by-field.

✍️

Name transliteration errors cause application delays

Diacritics, non-Latin scripts, and legal name matching between documents are among the most common causes of USCIS RFEs. We handle every script and match names precisely across documents.

Frequently asked questions

Birth certificate translation — questions answered.

Does USCIS accept your birth certificate translations?

Yes. Our certified translations meet USCIS 8 CFR 103.2(b)(3) requirements, which mandate a signed translator competency declaration. We have processed thousands of birth certificate translations for I-485, I-130, N-400, and other USCIS petitions without a single certification-based rejection.

What's included in the certification statement?

Our certification statement includes the translator's full name, ATA certification number, a declaration of competency in both the source and target languages, and a statement that the translation is complete and accurate to the best of their knowledge. This meets USCIS, court, and federal agency requirements.

My birth certificate is 2 pages — is that 2 pages of pricing?

Yes — pricing is per page of the original document. A 2-page birth certificate is billed at 2 pages ($49.98 at standard rate). If your certificate has a front and back on a single sheet, that counts as 2 pages.

How do I submit a birth certificate that's handwritten?

Upload a clear scan or high-resolution photograph. Our translators are experienced with handwritten civil registry entries from Mexico, Central America, and other countries where handwritten certificates are common. If the handwriting is partially illegible, we will note it in the translation.

How long does it take?

Standard delivery is 24–72 hours. Rush service (12–24 hours, +25%) is available for most language pairs. For same-day urgent needs, contact us directly — we maintain on-call translators for Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic, Vietnamese, and French.
