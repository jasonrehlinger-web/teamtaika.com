Effective April 24, 2026

ADA Title II — WCAG 2.1 AA

State and local governments must ensure all websites and mobile apps conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. This is NOW in effect. We audit, remediate, and certify conformance.

Federal ICT

Section 508 — Rehabilitation Act

All ICT developed or procured by federal agencies must be accessible. Every federal solicitation requires a completed VPAT/ACR. We create and maintain VPATs for vendors and contractors.

Effective May 2026

Section 504 — Healthcare

Healthcare organizations receiving federal funding must comply with new Section 504 digital accessibility requirements by May 11, 2026. We audit patient portals, forms, and clinical documents.

International Standard

WCAG 2.2 AA

The current W3C standard (September 2023), now an ISO standard. Adds 9 new success criteria beyond WCAG 2.1. Organizations serving global or diverse populations should target 2.2 AA.

Documents

PDF/UA — ISO 14289

The international standard for universally accessible PDFs. Required for any PDF document distributed to the public by organizations subject to Section 508 or ADA Title II.

Education

IDEA — Digital Accessibility

IEP documents, parent notifications, and school communications must be accessible to parents with disabilities. We remediate education documents and train district staff on accessible document creation.

Compliance questions answered

Section 508 & ADA accessibility FAQ.

The April 24, 2026 deadline applies to all state and local government entities — including school districts, county agencies, city departments, public universities, and any organization that is a "public entity" under ADA Title II. If you receive state or local government funding or operate as a government agency, this rule applies to you. Federal agencies are covered separately under Section 508.
Section 508 applies specifically to federal agencies and their contractors — it governs ICT (information and communication technology) procured or developed with federal funds. ADA Title II applies to state and local government entities and was updated in April 2026 to explicitly require WCAG 2.1 Level AA for websites and mobile apps. Both standards now reference WCAG, meaning conformance to WCAG 2.1 AA satisfies both for most digital content.
A VPAT (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) is a document in which a vendor describes how their product or service conforms to Section 508 standards. Federal agencies are required to request and review VPATs (called Accessibility Conformance Reports, or ACRs) before purchasing ICT products or services. If you sell products or services to federal agencies, you need a VPAT for each product. We create and maintain VPATs for vendors, contractors, and government agencies.
The most important action is to begin documented remediation immediately and establish a remediation plan on file. Enforcement agencies distinguish between organizations with no action taken and those actively working toward compliance. Contact us today — we can complete a preliminary assessment within 24 hours and deliver a full remediation plan within the week. Acting now, even after a deadline, is always better than continued inaction.
A single PDF can be assessed within 24 hours and remediated within 48 hours for most standard documents. Larger volumes are batched: 50 pages typically takes 3–5 business days; 200+ pages is priced and scoped individually. We prioritize your most-accessed or highest-risk documents first, so compliance gains are visible immediately even for large portfolios.
