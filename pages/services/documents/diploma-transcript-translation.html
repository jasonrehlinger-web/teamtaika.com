Why academic credential translation requires specialists

WES, licensing boards, and admissions offices have specific requirements — a general translation won't pass review.

Academic credential evaluation organizations and state licensing boards have strict standards for translated documents. The wrong format or missing content can delay your credential evaluation by months.

🎓

WES requires certified translations from all credential evaluation applicants

World Education Services (WES) and other credential evaluation organizations require ATA-certified translations of all foreign academic documents. Our translations meet WES's specific requirements and are accepted without revision.

🏥

Professional licensing boards require translated diplomas

State licensing boards for medicine, engineering, accounting, and nursing require translated foreign diplomas before issuing US professional licenses. Our translators understand the specific terminology and formatting each board expects.

📊

Foreign transcripts use unfamiliar grading formats

Academic transcripts from some countries list coursework in formats, grading scales, and institutional structures that are unfamiliar to US admissions offices. Our translators add explanatory notes when needed to clarify grading scales and degree equivalencies.

Frequently asked questions

Diploma and transcript translation — questions answered.

Does WES accept your diploma and transcript translations?

Yes. Our certified translations meet WES's requirements for ATA-certified translations. We have processed hundreds of credential evaluation packages for WES, ECE, NCES, and other NACES member organizations. Our certification format and credentials meet their standards without revision.

My transcript is 8 pages — do I have to translate all of it?

WES and most credential evaluation organizations require a complete translation of all transcript pages, including the grading scale and legend page. Selective translation is generally not accepted. We will translate the complete document and let you know the exact page count and cost before we begin.

What if my diploma is in Chinese characters?

Chinese (Simplified and Traditional) is one of our highest-volume language pairs for academic credentials. Our Chinese academic translators specialize in credentials from mainland China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, and other Chinese-language educational systems — each of which has different institutional naming conventions.

Do you translate the university's official seal description?

Yes. We translate all content on the diploma including the institutional seal description, degree name, field of study, conferral date, and any signatures or official notations. WES and licensing boards expect a complete translation — we deliver one.

Can I use this for NCEES engineering licensing?

Yes. The National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying (NCEES) requires certified translations of foreign engineering diplomas and transcripts for license applications. Our translations meet NCEES requirements and are accepted by state engineering licensing boards.
