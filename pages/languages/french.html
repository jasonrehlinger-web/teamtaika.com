The Stakes

Why getting French translation right matters

French documents from France, Canada, West Africa, and the Caribbean each carry regional vocabulary and legal conventions. A one-size-fits-all approach risks errors that can invalidate immigration filings, legal agreements, or medical records.

Common consequences of poor translation

  • USCIS rejections from imprecise legal terminology
  • Legal contract disputes from translation errors across French variants
  • Healthcare miscommunication for Haitian Creole-speaking patients
  • Title VI violations in communities with significant French-speaking populations
