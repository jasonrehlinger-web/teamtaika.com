Why accuracy isn't optional

One mistranslated word in a legal document
can cost more than the entire project.

Government agencies and school districts face a specific translation problem: the documents that need translation are also the documents where errors carry legal, financial, or human consequences.

⚖️

Legal documents

A single mistranslated term in a contract, court record, or regulatory filing can invalidate the document or create liability. Our legal translators hold subject-matter credentials — not just language fluency.

🏥

Medical records

Clinical notes, discharge summaries, and consent forms must be medically accurate in both languages. We assign medical translators with clinical terminology expertise — and HIPAA compliance baked in.

📋

USCIS & immigration

USCIS requires certified translations with a translator competency declaration. Ours are accepted by USCIS, state courts, academic institutions, and federal agencies without exception.

Frequently asked questions

Translation questions, answered.

A certified translation includes a signed statement from the translator declaring they are competent in both languages and that the translation is complete and accurate. USCIS requires this for all foreign-language documents submitted in immigration petitions. Our certifications are accepted by USCIS, federal courts, state agencies, academic institutions, and most international bodies without additional authentication.
Standard delivery is 24–72 hours depending on document length and complexity. Rush service (12–24 hours, +25%) and same-day urgent service (+50%) are available for most language pairs. Contact us directly for extremely urgent needs — we maintain on-call translators for several high-demand languages including Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic, Vietnamese, and French.
Yes. We hold an active GSA Schedule contract and NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contract. Government agencies can issue a task order directly against either vehicle — no separate competitive procurement required. Contact us with your agency type and state and we will confirm your specific ordering path the same day.
Subject-matter specialists only. A legal translator working on a contract is different from the medical translator working on a clinical record. All translators in our network are ATA-certified (or hold equivalent national credentials) and are assigned based on their documented expertise in your specific document type, not just their language pair.
We support more than 300 language pairs including all major world languages (Spanish, Mandarin, French, Arabic, Portuguese, Russian, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, Hindi, Tagalog, and many more) as well as indigenous languages, signed languages, and rare dialects. For uncommon languages, we will confirm availability and timeline before accepting the project.
All translators sign confidentiality agreements before receiving any document. Our secure portal uses TLS 1.3 encryption for transmission and AES-256 for storage. We are HIPAA compliant and FERPA compliant. For classified or highly sensitive documents, we can accommodate additional security requirements — contact us to discuss your specific needs.
